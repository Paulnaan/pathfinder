How the ranking works
Each result is ranked by Claude weighing several factors against your profile. It is not a league table of famous schools — it is a fit score for you specifically. A less-famous institution can rank #1 if it matches your budget, country, specialisation and eligibility better than a prestigious one that doesn't.
Specialisation match
Does the course actually teach what you selected? A general film degree ranks lower than a specialist cinematography programme if that's what you chose.
Budget fit
Courses within your budget rank higher. Ones that stretch or exceed it are shown but flagged — useful to know they exist even if not affordable now.
Eligibility match
Your age, qualifications, English level and portfolio are checked against what each institution typically requires. "Likely met" ranks higher than "check required".
Country preference
Institutions in your selected countries rank higher. If "Anywhere" is selected, country weighting is neutral.
Institution type
Types you selected are prioritised. If you chose Film School and University, both are considered equally — unselected types only appear if the shortlist would otherwise be too thin.
Profile interests
What you wrote in the free-text interests field is read carefully. A student who mentioned documentary and social issues will see schools with strong documentary programmes ranked higher.
What to do after getting results
Step 1 — Read all 7, don't just look at #1
The #1 result is best fit on paper. But #3 or #4 might have something specific — a tutor you want to learn from, a location that suits you — that changes your decision. Read all 7 before shortlisting.
Step 2 — Open every "Visit" link and spend 10 minutes on each site
Look for: graduate showreels (Vimeo/YouTube), current student work, who the tutors are and what they've made, equipment lists, and whether the course content looks recently updated.
Step 3 — Use Compare on your top 3
Click ⊕ Compare on any 3 cards, then go to the Compare tab. You'll see all 8 meters side-by-side, plus tuition and eligibility — useful for a final decision conversation with family.
Step 4 — Tick off the Checklist items for your shortlisted schools
Go to the Checklist tab. It covers open days, portfolio prep, documents, and financial planning. Each item saves as you tick it — it persists even if you close the browser.
Step 5 — Run a second search with different filters
Change one thing — try a different country, a lower budget, or switch from Undergraduate to Foundation. You'll see how the shortlist shifts and discover options you might not have considered.
Step 6 — Contact institutions directly
For international applicants, email the admissions office before applying. Ask: what is the IELTS requirement, when is the application deadline, is there a portfolio submission, and are there scholarships for international students.
What to look for in a course
🏛️
Accreditation
UK: HESA-registered, BAFTA-accredited. USA: NAST, regional accreditors. India: UGC/AICTE. Germany: state-accredited Kunsthochschule. Qatar: QAA or Ministry of Education.
🎯
Industry pipeline
Alumni credits on IMDB. Partnerships with BBC, Channel 4, Netflix, MBC, DW. Active placement programmes. Check LinkedIn for where graduates actually work.
✋
Practical vs theory ratio
Some courses are 70% theory. If you want to make films — not just study them — look for programs where students are on set from year 1. The curriculum note in each result card tells you this.
🎬
Facilities & equipment
What cameras do they teach on? Is there a live studio, DI suite, Dolby mix room? Equipment access often matters more than the institution's name.
📍
Location & safety
London, LA, Mumbai, Berlin, Doha — being near a production hub means real internships. Research the city's cost of living, safety record, and public transport before committing.
📈
Industry currency
Is the syllabus updated? Do tutors work in the industry today? Old courses teach obsolete workflows. Check when the course description was last revised.
🌐
Language of teaching
Germany and France have world-class film schools that teach in the local language. Confirm the primary language of lectures, assessments, and industry contacts before applying.
💸
True total cost
Tuition is just part of it. Add accommodation, food, equipment, software, travel to shoots, and visa costs. London adds ~£12–18k/yr living cost on top of tuition. Use the "include living costs" toggle in the search.
UK undergraduate application timeline
September — 16 months before entry
Start building your portfolio seriously
Make short films, even on your phone. Story and composition beat expensive kit. Start a Vimeo or portfolio site for your work.
October — 15 months before
Open UCAS, research shortlist, attend open days
UCAS opens for next cycle. Open days run Oct–Nov. You get 5 choices. Use PATH FINDER to shortlist before committing.
January 29 — Main UCAS deadline
All undergraduate applications submitted
Personal statement (4000 chars), predicted grades, reference. Critical deadline — don't miss it.
February – May
Interviews, portfolio reviews, auditions
Film schools often interview. Some want a short film submission, written treatment, or in-person portfolio day. Check each school specifically.
May – July
Accept offers — firm and insurance choices
Conditional offers tied to exam results. Firm = first choice. Insurance = backup. Both lock in via UCAS.
August — Results day
Confirmation or Clearing
Met your offer = confirmed. Didn't = Clearing opens immediately. Act fast, call universities directly.
Key scholarships to research
🏅
BAFTA scholarships (UK)
Means-tested, competitive. Apply separately from your UCAS application. For UK students at partner film schools.
🌍
Chevening (international → UK)
UK government postgraduate scholarships. 160+ countries eligible. Covers full fees and living costs. Extremely competitive.
🇮🇳
FTII and SRFTI (India)
Government institutions in Pune and Kolkata. Heavily subsidised — among the best value film education routes in the world.
🎓
Fulbright (USA)
US government scholarship for international students. Covers nearly everything. Very competitive, apply a year in advance.
🕌
Qatar Foundation
QF scholarships for students in Qatar and regionally. Education City Doha hosts several international university branches with dedicated funding.
🇩🇪
DAAD (Germany)
German Academic Exchange Service — scholarships for international students to study in Germany. Many German film schools (HFF, DFFB) charge no tuition at all.
